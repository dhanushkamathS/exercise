🥊 4-Round Shadow Boxing Session

  • 1Warm-up: shoulder rolls, hip circles, light jogging in place3 min
  • 2Round 1: Jab-cross combos, footwork, stay light on feet2 min
  • 3Rest — walk, shake out arms1 min
  • 4Round 2: Add hooks + slips. Move around, don't stand still2 min
  • 5Rest1 min
  • 6Round 3: Freestyle — throw combos, practice defence, stay bouncy2 min
  • 7Rest1 min
  • 8Round 4: Max effort — push pace, mix combos, imagine opponent2 min
  • 9Cool-down: slow walk, deep breaths2 min

💡 Keep fists lightly clenched, don't over-grip. Right wrist should stay neutral on impact — imagine pushing with the palm heel, not snapping the wrist.

📈 How to Progress Over Time

  • Add 1–2 reps per set each week before increasing weight. Reach the top rep target before upping the load.
  • For ring exercises, adjust body angle to increase or decrease difficulty (more horizontal = harder).
  • Use a notebook or phone note to log sets/reps each session — this is your #1 tool for muscle growth.
  • After 4 weeks, switch up one exercise per muscle group to keep it fresh and prevent adaptation.
  • Shadow boxing: increase rounds from 4 → 5 → 6 over weeks. Add intensity inside rounds progressively.
  • Right wrist: if discomfort returns, immediately switch to band alternatives and take 3–5 days off that movement.
  • Weekend = rest. Optional: a 20-min walk or gentle yoga session is fine, but no structured training.
